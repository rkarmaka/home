Welcome to my page!

"Innovation lies at the intersection of science, purpose, and people."

Welcome, and thanks for visiting! I’m Ranit Karmakar, a researcher, innovator, and entrepreneur with a deep commitment to building technologies that make a meaningful difference in the world. My journey has spanned multiple sectors — from automating enterprise workflows in industry to deploying cutting-edge AI systems for healthcare and biology in academic labs. Throughout, I’ve sought to combine scientific rigor with real-world impact, working at the confluence of machine learning, medicine, and human-centered design.

Currently based at Harvard University, I work at the intersection of applied machine learning, translational medical AI, and academic strategy. In this role, I contribute to high-impact research, co-author grants, mentor students, and help shape partnerships that bridge academia and industry. I’m especially drawn to problems that demand both deep technical expertise and strategic thinking — whether that’s scaling ML models, designing AI products for the clinic, or advising on future directions in AI research and innovation.

I believe that great science should not only be publishable, but usable — and that technology, when grounded in empathy and context, can drive transformative change.

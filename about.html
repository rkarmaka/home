About Me

Hi, I’m Ranit Karmakar, PhD — a researcher, innovator, and technical strategist working at the intersection of machine learning, medical AI, and scientific impact.

My journey has been defined by a consistent drive to apply cutting-edge technology to real-world problems. Over the past decade, I’ve worked across academia, startups, and industry, contributing to everything from scaling automation infrastructure to deploying AI for complex biomedical challenges.

Currently, I’m based at Harvard University, where I wear multiple hats — as a postdoctoral researcher, strategic advisor, educator, and collaborator. My work focuses on developing applied machine learning methods for healthcare and life sciences, particularly in areas like bioimage analysis, computer vision, and clinical AI. I also support academic leadership in grant writing, interdisciplinary research development, and academic-industry engagement.

What I do

Research Interest

My research is rooted in applied machine learning, with an emphasis on real-world deployment. During my PhD and current postdoc, I’ve developed models for biomedical image interpretation, clinical decision support, and translational AI applications. I’m especially interested in:

  • Small model design and LLM distillation
  • Weak supervision & self-supervised learning in medical imaging
  • Human-in-the-loop systems for clinical use
  • Scaling and infrastructure for AI-driven research

My Philosophy

I believe technology should be built with intention — not just to publish papers, but to solve meaningful problems. Whether I’m writing Python, drafting a grant, or mentoring a student, I aim to bridge the gap between research and reality.

A few principles I work by:

  • Build fast, but build responsibly
  • Cross boundaries. Code is most powerful when it's contextual
  • Empower others — the best work is rarely done alone
